The Analytics Gap in Mid-Market Companies

Mid-sized Nordic companies (1,000-5,000 employees) face a critical analytics capability gap that creates both operational challenges and strategic risks. They collect extensive HR data but lack the expertise to transform it into actionable insights.

Current Challenges

  • Too small to justify 2-3 FTE analytics hires (€200K+ annually)
  • Too complex for DIY with fragmented HR systems across HRIS, payroll, ATS
  • Too important to ignore with mounting regulatory pressure and compliance requirements
  • Too expensive with Big 4 consultants charging €200K+ for projects

Market Dynamics Driving Urgency

  • CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) affecting 50,000+ EU companies
  • EU pay transparency directive requiring detailed compensation reporting
  • Nordic talent scarcity making retention and workforce planning critical
  • Post-COVID transformation driving demand for data-driven HR decisions

Ideal Customer Profile & Stakeholder Mapping

Our ideal customers are Nordic companies with 1,000-5,000 employees who have outgrown basic HR reporting but lack the scale to justify building internal analytics teams. These organizations typically have multiple HR systems and face increasing pressure for data-driven decisions.

Company Characteristics

  • Size: 1,000-5,000 employees (sweet spot for fractional analytics)
  • Geography: Nordic headquarters (Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland)
  • Systems: Multiple HR platforms (HRIS + Payroll + ATS + Survey tools)
  • Analytics Maturity: Basic reporting but no dedicated analytics team
  • Growth Stage: Scaling or undergoing digital transformation
  • Compliance Needs: Subject to CSRD or EU pay transparency requirements

Key Stakeholders & Decision Process

  • Champion: HR Director/VP People (problem owner, daily user)
  • Economic Buyer: CFO (budget approval, ROI focus)
  • Executive Sponsor: CEO (strategic alignment, company-wide impact)
  • Primary Users: Global HRBPs (data consumers, insight translators)
  • Influencers: IT (technical requirements), Legal (compliance), Works Council (employee data privacy)

Typical Decision Timeline

Our target customers typically evaluate HR analytics solutions over 4-6 weeks, with the HR Director initiating the process, CFO evaluating ROI, and CEO providing final approval. The decision is often triggered by compliance deadlines or board-level requests for workforce insights.

Structured 4-6 Week Sales Process

Our sales process is designed for enterprise B2B purchasing cycles, with clear phases that build trust, demonstrate value, and address stakeholder concerns. The timeline typically spans 4-6 weeks from initial contact to signed contract.

Weeks 1-2: Discovery & Assessment

  • Initial Call with HR Director (1 hour): Problem identification and solution alignment
  • Data Landscape Assessment: Current systems, data quality, and integration requirements
  • Pain Point Deep Dive: Specific challenges with current analytics and reporting
  • Compliance Requirement Review: CSRD, pay transparency, and other regulatory needs

Weeks 3-4: Solution Design & Demo

  • Customized Demo with Sample Data: Show modules relevant to their specific needs
  • ROI Calculation Presentation: Cost comparison vs. alternatives and value realization timeline
  • Stakeholder Alignment Meeting: Include CFO, IT, and other decision influencers
  • Reference Calls: Connect with similar-sized Nordic clients if requested

Weeks 5-6: Closing & Implementation Planning

  • Contract Negotiation: Service tier selection, pricing terms, and SLA agreement
  • Legal and Procurement Review: Address data privacy, security, and compliance requirements
  • Implementation Planning: Timeline, resource allocation, and success metrics definition
  • Kickoff Scheduling: Set implementation start date and initial training sessions
